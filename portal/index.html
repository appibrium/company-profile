Color Palette Guidelines

  • Night & Deep are the primary dark canvasses. Dark interfaces should use Night for the main background and Deep for cards/elevated layers.
  • Mint is the primary energetic driver and logo color. Use for call-to-actions, active links, and focal graphic elements.
  • Snow is the canvas for light interfaces. It provides a soft, mint-tinted snow color that eliminates harsh white glare.

DO: High contrast lockups
Use the dark lockup on Night, Deep Forest, or dark-themed interfaces for solid contrast.
DON'T: Poor background contrast
Never place the white-wordmark dark lockup on light backgrounds or low-contrast containers.
DO: Clear space allocation
Keep at least 25% height-equivalent padding zone around all standalone icons and logotypes.
DON'T: Stretch or distort assets
Never modify or squeeze the logo aspects. Scale them proportionally to preserve geometric ratios.

Systematic Layout Standards

Appibrium's visual balance is engineered using mathematically proportionate layout templates. Below are standard guidelines:

  • W4 Lockup usage: The editorial block lockup (stacked descriptor left of vertical bar) is highly recommended for tech whitepapers, publication headers, and documentation covers.
  • Accent Ratio: Keep Electric Mint accent color (#00E090) capped at 10-15% of your total interface layout to ensure high aesthetic premium value.
  • App Icons: Use the standalone mint icon for mobile dashboards and app store squares. Use the outline variant for secondary or watermark widgets.
  • Horizontal alignment: Always left-align typography when utilizing standard lockups. Do not center tagline segments independently of name spans.
